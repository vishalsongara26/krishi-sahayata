Wheat

Wheat is a Rabi season crop, typically sown in November and harvested in March/April. It is one of India's staple food grains and a major source of nutrition.

Crop Duration

110-130 days from sowing to harvest

Water Requirements

Requires 4-6 irrigations. Critical stages: Crown root initiation, Tillering, Jointing, Flowering, Milk stage. Total water requirement: 450-650 mm.

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen: 120-150 kg/ha in 2-3 splits
  • Phosphorus: 60-80 kg/ha as basal dose
  • Potassium: 40-60 kg/ha
  • Zinc: 25 kg/ha zinc sulfate (if deficient)

Common Diseases

  • Rust: Use resistant varieties, spray Propiconazole
  • Karnal bunt: Seed treatment with carbendazim
  • Powdery mildew: Sulfur dusting or wettable sulfur
  • Leaf blight: Mancozeb spray at initial appearance

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soil with pH 6.0-7.5. Avoid waterlogged conditions. Good organic matter content preferred.

Climate

Cool weather with temperature between 10-25°C during growth. Requires sunny days during ripening. Frost sensitive during flowering.

Harvesting

Harvest when grains are hard and moisture content is 20-25%. Thresh immediately after harvesting to avoid shattering losses.

Yield Potential

Average yield: 4-5 tons/ha with improved varieties. Potential yield: 6-7 tons/ha under optimal conditions.

Rice

Rice is a Kharif season crop, typically sown in June-July and harvested in October-November. It is the staple food for a majority of Indians and requires substantial water resources.

Crop Duration

90-150 days depending on variety (short, medium, long duration)

Water Requirements

Requires standing water (5-10 cm) during most growth stages. Total water requirement: 1100-1250 mm. Critical stages: Tillering, Panicle initiation, Flowering.

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen: 100-150 kg/ha in 3-4 splits
  • Phosphorus: 50-60 kg/ha as basal dose
  • Potassium: 40-60 kg/ha
  • Zinc: 25 kg/ha zinc sulfate (if deficient)

Common Diseases

  • Blast: Use resistant varieties, spray Tricyclazole
  • Brown spot: Seed treatment with carbendazim
  • Sheath blight: Validamycin spray
  • Bacterial leaf blight: Streptocycline spray

Soil Requirements

Clayey loam with good water retention capacity. pH range: 5.5-6.5. Can tolerate slightly acidic conditions.

Climate

Hot and humid climate with temperature between 20-40°C. Requires high rainfall or irrigation. Sensitive to low temperatures.

Harvesting

Harvest when 80-85% grains turn yellow and become hard. Moisture content should be 20-25%. Dry to 12-14% moisture for storage.

Yield Potential

Average yield: 3-4 tons/ha. Potential yield: 6-8 tons/ha with hybrid varieties and proper management.

Maize

Maize is grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons. It's a versatile crop used for human consumption, animal feed, and industrial purposes.

Crop Duration

85-100 days for early varieties, 100-110 days for medium, 110-120 days for late varieties

Water Requirements

Requires 500-800 mm water. Critical stages: Knee-high stage, Tasseling, Silking, Grain filling. Sensitive to waterlogging.

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen: 120-150 kg/ha in 2-3 splits
  • Phosphorus: 60-80 kg/ha as basal
  • Potassium: 40-60 kg/ha
  • Zinc: 25 kg/ha if deficient

Common Diseases

  • Turcicum leaf blight: Mancozeb spray
  • Maydis leaf blight: Zineb or Mancozeb
  • Stalk rot: Avoid waterlogging
  • Common rust: Sulfur-based fungicides

Soil Requirements

Well-drained fertile loams and alluvial soils. pH: 6.5-7.5. Good organic matter content essential.

Climate

Warm weather crop. Temperature: 21-27°C. Requires frost-free period. Sensitive to low temperatures.

Harvesting

Harvest when grains are hard and moisture content is 20-25%. Dry to 12-14% for storage.

Yield Potential

Average yield: 3-4 tons/ha. Potential: 8-10 tons/ha with hybrids and proper management.

Barley

Barley is a Rabi season cereal crop known for its short duration and tolerance to saline and alkaline soils. Used for food, feed, and malt production.

Crop Duration

110-130 days depending on variety. Early maturing varieties: 90-100 days

Water Requirements

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen: 60-80 kg/ha in 2 splits
  • Phosphorus: 40-50 kg/ha as basal
  • Potassium: 20-30 kg/ha
  • Zinc: 20 kg/ha if deficient

Common Diseases

  • Stripe rust: Triadimefon spray
  • Powdery mildew: Sulfur dusting
  • Leaf blight: Mancozeb application
  • Covered smut: Seed treatment with carboxin

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soils. Tolerates saline and alkaline conditions better than wheat. pH: 6.5-8.0

Climate

Cool season crop. Temperature: 12-25°C. More tolerant to frost and drought than wheat.

Harvesting

Harvest when grains are hard and moisture content is 20-22%. Timely harvest prevents shattering.

Yield Potential

Average yield: 2.5-3.5 tons/ha. Potential: 4-5 tons/ha with improved varieties.

Citrus includes oranges, lemons, limes and other related fruits. Perennial trees with high nutritional and economic value.

Crop Duration

Perennial trees, start bearing in 3-5 years, productive for 25-30 years

Water Requirements

Requires 15-20 irrigations/year. Critical stages: Flowering, Fruit set, Fruit development

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen: 500-800 g/tree/year in splits
  • Phosphorus: 200-300 g/tree/year
  • Potassium: 300-500 g/tree/year
  • Zinc: Foliar spray if deficient

Common Diseases

  • Citrus canker: Copper-based sprays
  • Greening: Control psyllid vector
  • Powdery mildew: Sulfur dusting
  • Root rot: Proper drainage

Soil Requirements

Well-drained medium to light soils. Avoid heavy clay and calcareous soils. pH: 5.5-7.5

Climate

Subtropical fruit. Temperature: 13-37°C. Sensitive to frost and extreme heat.

Harvesting

Harvest based on color development and TSS:acid ratio. Multiple pickings for optimal quality.

Yield Potential

Average yield: 15-25 tons/ha. Potential: 40-50 tons/ha with improved varieties.

Coconut

Coconut is a perennial palm crop, often called 'Kalpavriksha' for its multiple uses. Grown in coastal regions and high rainfall areas.

Crop Duration

Perennial palm, starts bearing in 5-7 years, productive for 60-70 years

Water Requirements

High water requirement. Annual rainfall: 1500-2500 mm ideal. Critical stages: Dry months for irrigation

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen: 500 g/palm/year in 2 splits
  • Phosphorus: 300 g/palm/year
  • Potassium: 1200 g/palm/year
  • Organic manure: 50 kg/palm/year

Common Diseases

  • Root wilt: No effective control
  • Leaf rot: Bordeaux mixture
  • Bud rot: Remove affected tissues
  • Stem bleeding: Chisel and apply Bordeaux paste

Soil Requirements

Well-drained sandy loam to laterite soils. Good drainage essential. pH: 5.0-8.0

Climate

Tropical crop. Temperature: 20-32°C. High humidity and coastal climate preferred.

Harvesting

Harvest tender nuts at 6-7 months, mature nuts at 12 months. 45-60 day harvesting intervals.

Yield Potential

Average yield: 80-100 nuts/palm/year. Potential: 150-200 nuts/palm/year with good management.

Crop Duration

  • Potassium: 80-100 kg/ha

  • Rhizome rot: Proper drainage

  • Nematodes: Soil treatment

Climate

Harvesting

Harvest when leaves turn yellow and dry. Carefully dig out rhizomes to avoid damage.

Yield Potential
